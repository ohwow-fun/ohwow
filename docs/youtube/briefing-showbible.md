# Show Bible — The Briefing

> **Status:** generated by `yt-series-brief.mjs` on 2026-04-17, then
> human-tightened against the prompt module's actual format contract.
> The prompt module in `src/integrations/youtube/series/script-prompts/briefing-prompt.ts`
> is the executable source of truth. This doc is the narrative companion
> that explains **why** the prompt is shaped the way it is.

**Role:** Authority
**Tagline:** "Tell me what matters without wasting my time."
**Audience:** OHWOW.FUN viewers — founders, operators, AI-curious pros.

---

## 1. One-Liner

A **45-second daily AI-news Short** that tells one busy operator what
changed today and why it matters for their business this week.

One story. One actor. One artifact. One implication. One takeaway.

---

## 2. The 3-Second Identity

A viewer must know this is The Briefing within three seconds — phone
face-down on the coffee table, thumb scrolling. The Glance Test fails
if the first frame could also be a Tomorrow Broke episode.

What makes it unmistakable:

- **Palette:** light surface (`#f4f7fb` background, `#0a1629` text,
  `#2563eb` accent). Bright and awake, not cinematic or dark. Sets us
  apart from every other AI-news channel that defaults to black-on-neon.
- **Typography:** Merriweather (serif) for the hook line, Inter for
  body copy. The serif is the single most distinctive signal — nothing
  else on the for-you page uses a serif headline for tech news.
- **Opening frame:** the hook line is on screen at frame 1. No cold
  open, no countdown, no logo reveal. The viewer sees "Anthropic shipped
  Opus 4.7." before the voiceover finishes its first word.

What we're **not** doing (despite templates that suggest we should):
sonic logo, episode numbers, date stamps, host face, newsroom intro
sting. All of those eat seconds we don't have in a 45s format and
none of them differentiate — they just copy TV news into portrait.

---

## 3. Voice

**Tone:** newsroom-anchor credibility with operator-speak directness.
Confident, paced, no hedging. Assume the viewer has been watching the
AI space for two years — don't explain what an LLM is.

**Pace:** 155–170 WPM. Faster than conversational, slower than frantic.
Strategic 0.3s pauses between scenes, never within a sentence.

**Closest working reference:** the crisp-fact delivery of a Bloomberg
morning rundown, but in a founder's vocabulary. Not a talking-head
podcast vibe; this is a download.

**What to avoid in delivery:**
- Hedges: "experts say", "some argue", "it's possible that"
- Corporate superlatives: "game-changer", "paradigm shift", "revolutionary"
- Filler openers: "let's dive in", "in this video", "today we're looking at"
- Host-first framing: "I read that…", "in my opinion…"

---

## 4. Format Contract

**Total runtime:** 45s exactly. 30fps = 1350 frames.
**Narration target:** 105–135 words (≈2.5–3 WPS at our pace).

Four scenes, matching the prompt module's `hook / fact / implication /
takeaway` arc:

| Scene | Time | Frames | Words | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **hook** | 0–3s | 90 | 8–12 | Actor + artifact. One sentence. Text on screen from frame 1. |
| **fact** | 3–20s | 510 | 40–55 | The details that matter. Real names, real versions, real numbers. 2–3 sentences. |
| **implication** | 20–40s | 600 | 45–60 | Operator lens. What does this change, for whom, on what timeframe? One sentence. |
| **takeaway** | 40–45s | 150 | 10–15 | Action or watch-for. Not a sign-off. |

**Visual rule:** one scene kind per beat. Default mapping — `text-typewriter`
for hook (the headline types in), `composable` with grid-morph + scan-line
+ light-rays for fact and implication (newsroom-ticker energy), `quote-card`
for takeaway (single line, large, ends on a clean frame).

**When to flex the format:** we reserve the right to a 90s or 3min
extended cut when a story is legitimately bigger (major safety incident,
regulatory shift, $10B-plus raise). Extended cuts keep the same 4-beat
structure but expand `fact` and `implication` proportionally. Default is
45s; extended is the exception.

## 5. Source Rules

**Primary seed:** `x-intel-history.jsonl` rows with `bucket='advancements'`
from the last 24–48h, picked by the briefing seed adapter.

**Fallback:** researcher agent queries via `scrape_search` when no fresh
advancements rows exist.

**Seeds that make great Briefings:**
- Frontier model releases with a concrete change (version, price, limit).
- OSS repo launches that ship an actual binary or npm package.
- Regulatory moves with a named agency, docket, or bill number.
- Enterprise deploys with a named customer + dollar figure.
- Research papers where the abstract claim is surprising *and* testable.

**Seeds to skip:**
- Rumors without a citeable actor.
- "Could", "might", "is rumored to" — if it hasn't happened, it's not news.
- Stories already 48h+ old — algorithm doesn't reward stale.
- Anything where the implication reduces to "AI is changing fast."

---

## 6. Banned Phrases (Ship-Killers)

These live in the prompt module (`BRIEFING_BANNED_PHRASES`). Adding one
here means adding it to that array.

1. "everyone is talking about"
2. "game-changer" / "paradigm shift" / "revolutionary"
3. "in a world where…"
4. "unprecedented"
5. "experts say" / "some argue" / "many believe"
6. "it's possible that" / "could potentially"
7. "let's dive in"
8. "disrupting" / "disruption"
9. Asking a question you don't answer in the same sentence
10. Ending on "stay tuned" or "more coming soon"

---

## 7. Success Metric

**Primary — Average Percentage Viewed (APV)**
- Target: ≥70% at 45s length.
- Why: proves the pacing + density are right. Drop-off before 50%
  means the hook or the fact was weak.

**Secondary — Save Rate**
- Target: ≥1.5%.
- Why: operators saving a Short means it's worth returning to — the
  tactical takeaway worked.

**Tertiary — Subscriber conversion**
- Target: ≥0.3% view-to-subscribe.
- Why: The Briefing is the daily-viewer magnet. If single Briefings
  don't convert, the whole channel's retention arc is broken.

## 8. 10 Hook Templates

Adapted from what lands on AI-news YouTube Shorts in 2026. Not every
template fits every story; pick the one the seed actually earns.

1. **Actor + artifact:** "Anthropic shipped Opus 4.7." — the flattest,
   sharpest opener. Works when the name recognition does the lifting.
2. **Before/after stat:** "Your Claude bill dropped 30% overnight."
3. **Quiet launch:** "OpenAI released this without a blog post."
4. **Counter-intuitive fact:** "The biggest AI story this week isn't
   from a frontier lab."
5. **Specific-number opener:** "1.4 billion tokens. Free. Starting today."
6. **Named-customer moment:** "Walmart just put Gemini in every store."
7. **Status change:** "Gemini is now the default assistant on
   Android."
8. **Negative implication:** "Your vector DB is about to get more
   expensive."
9. **Release-day exclusive:** "Here's what shipped in the last six
   hours."
10. **Policy shock:** "The EU AI Act just carved out open weights."

**Hooks to avoid regardless of story:**
- Rhetorical questions ("What if AI could…").
- "In this video I'll…".
- Slow reveal pan with delayed point.
- "You won't believe…" (or any clickbait opener).

## 9. 10 Failure Modes & Pre-Post Catchers

| # | Failure | Catcher |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Vague actor** | Hook doesn't name a specific company / lab / project. **Fix:** rewrite the hook around the actor. |
| 2 | **No artifact** | Hook mentions an actor but no specific thing shipped. **Fix:** name the version / product / paper. |
| 3 | **Generic implication** | "This changes everything" / "AI is moving fast". **Fix:** one operator lens, one timeframe. |
| 4 | **Takeaway is a sign-off** | "Thanks for watching" / "let me know what you think". **Fix:** swap for an action or watch-for. |
| 5 | **Hedges in the narration** | Count them. >1 = rewrite. **Fix:** replace each hedge with a concrete claim or cut the sentence. |
| 6 | **Word count out of band** | <100 or >140 for 45s. **Fix:** align scenes to audio duration via `alignSceneDurations`. |
| 7 | **Hook not visible at frame 1** | First frame is a logo, a fade-in, or black. **Fix:** force `text-typewriter` with `typingSpeed: 1.5` so letters land within 100ms. |
| 8 | **Takeaway that's a CTA** | "Subscribe for more". **Fix:** the takeaway is information, not a conversion ask. |
| 9 | **Two stories in one Short** | Narration has "also" or "meanwhile". **Fix:** pick the stronger one, ship the other tomorrow. |
| 10 | **OHWOW self-reference** | Banned-phrase check is global; if it trips, the seed was wrong — re-pick. |

## 10. Curriculum (10 Briefing-Shaped Ideas)

Seeds to look for when `x-intel` runs dry. Each is shaped so the
hook/fact/implication/takeaway structure has something to bite.

1. First open-weight model that passes a named enterprise benchmark
   we can cite.
2. Pricing-tier change at a frontier lab that shifts the per-token
   economics of a real workflow.
3. A vertical-AI startup with a named public customer logo + MRR.
4. Hardware launch with a benchmark we can compare to a known chip.
5. A regulatory action (EU, US, China) with a specific docket + date.
6. Agent-infra release that replaces a developer role (the specific
   role + the before/after time).
7. Evals data that contradicts the vibes (model everyone thinks is
   great underperforms; underdog outperforms).
8. Funding round above a threshold ($500M+) where we can name the
   lead + a concrete use of proceeds.
9. A deprecation / sunset that forces operators to migrate in <30
   days.
10. A named incident (outage, leak, breach, recall) with a named
    responsible party and a documented fix.

---

**Final note.** The Briefing is not a show. It's a morning habit. The
viewer is deciding in the first second whether to finish their scroll or
finish their coffee with us. Every frame has to earn the next one.
