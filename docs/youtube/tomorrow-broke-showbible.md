# Show Bible — Tomorrow Broke

> **Status:** skeleton. Run `node scripts/yt-experiments/yt-series-brief.mjs tomorrow-broke`
> to fill this doc with deep-research findings.

## One-liner

Cinematic AI-future micro-stories. Black Mirror meets mini-documentary.
Show a future — utopia, dystopia, unintended consequence — as if it's
already happened. Make it feel inevitable.

## Voice

Noir narrator. Deadpan, understated, slightly ominous. Observing something
everyone missed. Slower pace than Briefing; sentences breathe. First-
person rare; usually an observer who noticed.

## Format contract

**Length:** 30–60s primary; 2 min for deeper scenarios.

**Structure (60s default — 3 scenes):**

1. **Datestamp hook (0–4s)** — *"In 2034 nobody saw it coming."* A
   concrete year, a concrete place, a concrete moment.
2. **Scenario beat (4–35s)** — the world. Small, specific detail.
   Ordinary life bent by the technology. Not a lecture.
3. **Loop-landing (35–60s)** — final line recontextualizes the opener.
   Watch the loop: hook + loop = continuous thought.

## Source rules

Primary: x-intel `predictions` rows (falsifiable forecasts) + internal
scenario archive (`~/.ohwow/workspaces/<ws>/tomorrow-broke-scenarios.jsonl`
— created by the first drafter run).

Prefer specifics already in the world today: concrete companies, concrete
artifacts, specific jobs. Extrapolate carefully; never invent stats.

## Banned

- Vague futurism ("the AI age", "a new era")
- "Some say" / "many predict" hedging
- Dystopia-for-dystopia's-sake clickbait without a concrete mechanism
- Tech worship without consequence
- OHWOW self-references

## Success metric

Shares + comments. Target: ≥5% comment rate on ≥10k-view posts.

## Loop patterns

- **Close the circle** — final line answers the hook's tension in a way
  that makes the opening sound different.
- **Invert the hook** — final line makes the opener feel wrong.
- **Plant a word** — final line drops a phrase that only means something
  on rewatch.
