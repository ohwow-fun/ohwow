# BIOS — Biological/Life-Aware Computing

**Layer 7: Respect for the biological being.**

The system knows its human operators are not productivity machines. They have energy cycles, stress responses, recovery needs, and boundaries between work and life.

## Philosophy

- **Laozi (Wu Wei)**: sometimes the wisest action is inaction. Not everything needs optimizing.
- **Buddha (Karuna)**: compassion. Detect suffering and respond by reducing load, not adding to it.

## Modules

| Module | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| `energy-wave.ts` | Ultradian rhythm detection (~90 min cycles from activity intensity). Peak/rising/falling/trough. |
| `stress-detector.ts` | Behavioral signals: shorter messages, faster approvals (rushing), higher rejection rate. Calm/focused/pressured/stressed. |
| `recovery-advisor.ts` | After 5+ consecutive high-intensity days, recommends shifting to reflective work. |
| `boundary-guardian.ts` | Learns work-life boundaries from behavior. Enforces: no non-critical notifications during rest. |
| `notification-filter.ts` | Bio-aware timing: batch during troughs, suppress during boundaries, critical-only during stress. |

## The Key Principle

When the system detects a human is stressed, overloaded, or in a low-energy state, it doesn't add more tasks. It reduces notifications, batches approvals, and suggests the human take a break. Compassion is more productive than optimization.
